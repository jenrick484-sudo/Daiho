COOLING SYSTEM

The Cooling System in a vehicle regulates engine temperature to prevent overheating and ensure efficient operation. It removes excess heat generated during combustion and maintains optimal engine temperature for performance and longevity.

Common Issues in the Cooling System:

1. Coolant Leaks:
  • Caused by cracked hoses, radiator damage, faulty gaskets, or loose clamps.
2. Overheating Engine:
  • Result of low coolant, blocked radiator, malfunctioning thermostat, or failed water pump.
3. Clogged Radiator:
  • Debris or rust buildup reduces coolant flow and heat dissipation.
4. Faulty Thermostat:
  • Stuck closed: Prevents coolant flow, causing overheating.
  • Stuck open: Engine runs too cool, reducing efficiency.
5. Worn-out Water Pump:
  • Fails to circulate coolant properly, leading to overheating.
6. Air Pockets in the System:
  • Trapped air prevents proper coolant circulation, causing inconsistent cooling.

Maintenance Tips:

Tips no.1
  • Check coolant level and top up if needed and inspect for leaks around hoses, radiator, and water pump.
Tips no.2
  • Flush and replace coolant every 2-3 years or as recommended by the manufacturer to prevent corrosion and buildup.
Tips no.3
  • Replace if the engine is running unusually hot or cold.
Tips no.4
  • Watch for overheating signs on the dashboard.
Tips no.5
  • Listen for whining noise or coolant leaks from the pump area.
Tips no.6
  • Ensure the cap seals properly to maintain correct pressure in the system.
