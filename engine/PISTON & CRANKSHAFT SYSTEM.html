PISTON AND CRANKSHAFT SYSTEM

The Piston and Crankshaft System is a key part of the engine's internal mechanism. It converts the energy from fuel combustion into rotational motion, which powers the vehicle.

Common Issues in the Piston and Crankshaft System:

1. Worn Piston Rings
  • Oil consumption, loss of compression, engine smoke.
2. Crankshaft Bearing Failure
  • Knocking noises, low oil pressure, engine damage.
3. Cracked/Broken Pistons
  • Loss of power, engine misfire, severe engine damage.
4. Bent Connecting Rod
  • Engine vibration, poor performance, catastrophic failure.
5. Crankshaft Damage
  • Engine seizure, loud knocking, failure to start.

Maintenance Tips:

Tips no.1
  • Regularly change engine oil to prevent wear.
Tips no.2
  • Use high-quality oil and fuel.
Tips no.3
  • Avoid engine overheating.
Tips no.4
  • Do not overload or over-rev the engine.
Tips no.5
  • Perform compression tests during major services to check piston and ring health.
