CRANKCASE VENTILATION SYSTEM

The Crankcase Ventilation System is designed to manage and remove blow-by gases (unburned fuel, air, and combustion gases) that escape past the piston rings into the engineâ€™s crankcase. This system prevents pressure buildup, reduces harmful emissions, and recycles gases back into the combustion process.

Common Issues in the Crankcase Ventilation System:

1. Clogged PCV Valve:
  • Leads to pressure buildup, oil leaks, rough idling, or poor fuel economy.
2. Oil Contamination:
  • Dirty crankcase gases can cause sludge buildup and reduce engine performance.
3. Cracked or Leaking Hoses:
  • Air leaks can lead to vacuum issues, rough running, or increased emissions.
4. Excessive Blow-by:
  • Caused by worn piston rings or engine wear, leading to increased oil consumption and contamination.

Maintenance Tips:

Tips no.1
  • Check every 30,000 to 50,000 km or as recommended and replace if clogged, stuck, or leaking.
Tips no.2
  • Look for cracks, brittleness, or oil buildup; replace if damaged.
Tips no.3
  • Regular oil changes help prevent sludge and contamination buildup.
Tips no.4
  • Keep an eye on seals and gaskets for leaks caused by excess crankcase pressure.
