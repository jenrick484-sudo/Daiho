EXHAUST SYSTEM

The Exhaust System is responsible for safely directing and treating the gases produced during the combustion process in a vehicleâ€™s engine. It plays a crucial role in reducing harmful emissions, minimizing engine noise, and improving overall engine efficiency.

Common Issues in the Exhaust System:

1. Exhaust Leaks:
  • Cracked pipes or gaskets allow gases to escape before treatment, leading to noise and potential health hazards.
2. Clogged Catalytic Converter:
  • Causes reduced engine performance, poor fuel economy, and increased emissions.
3. Faulty Oxygen Sensors:
  • Results in improper fuel-air mixture, affecting efficiency and emissions.
4. Rust & Corrosion:
  • Common in exhaust pipes and mufflers, especially in humid or salty environments.
5. Loud Noises:
  • Caused by damaged mufflers or leaks in the exhaust system.

Maintenance Tips:

Tips no.1
  • Check for rust, cracks, loose components, and leaks.
Tips no.2
  • Pay attention to the Check Engine Light, especially if related to the catalytic converter or O2 sensors.
Tips no.3
  • Typically every 100,000 km or as recommended.
Tips no.4
  • Hissing, roaring, or rattling noises may indicate issues in the system.
Tips no.5
  • Wash undercarriage regularly, especially in winter or salty areas.
