LUBRICATION SYSTEM

The lubrication system in an internal combustion engine reduces friction, dissipates heat, and cleans internal engine components by circulating oil. Proper lubrication is essential for engine longevity and performance.

Common Issues in the lubrication System:

1. Low Oil Level
  • Insufficient lubrication, leading to increased friction, overheating, and engine damage.
2. Oil Leaks
  • Oil pan gasket, valve cover gasket, crankshaft seals, oil filter, or drain plug.
3. Clogged Oil Filter
  • Dirt, sludge, or debris buildup over time
4. Dirty or Contaminated Oil
  • Poor lubrication, increased engine wear, sludge formation.
