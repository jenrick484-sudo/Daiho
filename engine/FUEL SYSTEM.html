FUEL SYSTEM

The fuel system stores, filters, and delivers fuel to the engine for combustion, ensuring the engine runs efficiently and smoothly.

Common Issues in the Fuel System:

1. Clogged Fuel Filter
  • Dirt, debris, and rust block fuel flow.
2. Faulty Fuel Pump
  • Worn-out fuel pump leads to inadequate fuel pressure.
3. Leaking Fuel Lines or Connections
  • Cracks or damage cause fuel leaks
4. Dirty or Clogged Fuel Injectors
  • Deposits restrict fuel spray pattern.
5. Fuel Pressure Regulator Failure
  • Inability to maintain correct fuel pressure.

Maintenance Tips:

Tips no.1
  • Use high-quality fuel to prevent injector deposits.
Tips no.2
  • Inspect fuel lines and connections for leaks.
Tips no.3
  • Clean fuel injectors periodically (if recommended).
Tips no.4
  • Monitor fuel pump noise (loud humming may signal wear).
Tips no.5
  • Check fuel cap condition (damaged caps cause vapor leaks).
